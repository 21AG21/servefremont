## The email (counselor)

**To:** [Counselor name], [School] counseling office — **Washington High
is the strongest first target**: their own "Opportunities for Community
Service" page already links out to the Age Well Center independently of
ServeFremont, which means whoever maintains that page already does this
work by hand and will immediately see what the site replaces.

**Subject:** A verified map of Fremont volunteer opportunities for
[School] students

> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm a [School] student, and I built ServeFremont — a map of volunteer
> opportunities in Fremont for students looking for service hours. I've
> personally visited or directly confirmed [N] organizations so far (not
> pulled from a national database), so everything on it is current and
> actually open to teens.
>
> Students can filter by age, schedule, whether it's walkable from
> [School], and whether the org signs school hour forms — the four things
> I kept hearing were hardest to figure out from scattered flyers and old
> lists. It's free, has no ads, and doesn't collect any student data (no
> accounts, nothing to sign up for).
>
> Would it be alright to link it from the counseling or CSF/NHS page? Happy
> to do a 5-minute walkthrough first if that's easier — whatever's less of
> your time.
>

> Arjun
> [site URL]

*One ask, made easy to say yes to: link placement, not a meeting requirement.
The 5-minute demo is offered, not pushed. No mention of the district or
FUSD by name in the body — that's what the About page's disclaimer is for,
not the cold open.*

## Adjusting for an admin (assistant principal / activities director) instead

Same email, three changes — admins weigh liability and district exposure
differently than a counselor weighing "is this a good resource":

1. **Open with independence, not features.** Add a line up front: "This is
   an independent student project, not affiliated with FUSD or the City of
   Fremont — that's stated on the site itself." Say it before they have to
   go looking for it.
2. **Name the safety floor explicitly.** One line: "No accounts, no student
   data collected, nothing for a student to log into — it's a filtered
   list of external links, same as any resource page." Admins' first
   question is usually "what are we exposing students to," not "is this
   useful."
3. **Lower the ask.** Don't request a link placement from an admin — ask
   for a forward to the right counselor/club adviser instead. Admins
   aren't usually the ones maintaining a resource page, and asking them to
   own a link creates approval friction the counselor ask doesn't have.
